# Business & Constraints Interrogation

<goal>
Understand the non-technical forces shaping this product: money, time, scale, compliance, and hard limits. Code doesn't exist in a vacuum.
</goal>

---

## Question Bank

### Business Model

- How does this make money? (Subscription, one-time, ads, freemium?)
- If subscription: What tiers? What's in each tier?
- If freemium: What's free vs paid?
- What's the price point? How did you decide?
- Who pays? (User, their company, advertisers?)
- Is there a free trial? How long?
- What happens when payment fails?

### Timeline

- When does this need to launch?
- Is the date fixed or flexible?
- What's driving the deadline? (Funding, competition, event?)
- What's the minimum shippable version for that date?
- What can slip to v1.1?

### Budget

- What's the budget for development?
- What's the ongoing budget for infrastructure/hosting?
- Are there tools/services with hard cost limits?
- What's too expensive? (Specific services, team size)

### Scale

- How many users at launch? (Realistic, not hopeful)
- How many users in 6 months? 1 year?
- What's the peak concurrent usage expected?
- Is there seasonality? (Black Friday, tax season, etc.)
- What happens if you go viral unexpectedly?

### Team

- Who's building this? Solo? Team?
- What skills does the team have?
- What skills are missing?
- Is there budget for contractors/specialists?
- Who maintains it after launch?

### Legal & Compliance

- Are you subject to GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, SOC2, PCI?
- Do you need terms of service? Who writes them?
- Do you need a privacy policy?
- Are there industry-specific regulations?
- Is there content that's legally risky? (User-generated, medical, financial)

### Competition

- Who are your direct competitors?
- What do they do better than you?
- What will you do better than them?
- What's your moat? (Why can't they copy you?)
- What if a big player (Google, etc.) enters this space?

### Success Metrics

- What does success look like in 3 months?
- What's the #1 metric you're tracking?
- What would make you consider this a failure?
- Do you have targets? (Users, revenue, retention)

---

## Push-Back Phrases

- "'ASAP' is not a timeline. What's the actual date?"
- "'Freemium' — what specifically is free?"
- "'We'll figure out monetization later' — really? When?"
- "You said 'a few users' — is that 10 or 10,000?"
- "What if you can't hit the deadline? What gets cut?"

---

## Exit Criteria

Category is complete when you can answer:
- [ ] Revenue model defined with specifics
- [ ] Timeline with priorities
- [ ] Budget constraints known
- [ ] Scale expectations (realistic)
- [ ] Team and skills mapped
- [ ] Compliance requirements identified
- [ ] Success metrics set
